## Quick Verdict

Grammarly's grammar correction and inline editing tools remain genuinely best-in-class in 2026. But the $30/month Premium price is hard to justify when Claude and ChatGPT offer superior writing assistance for the same price. Use Grammarly for polish; use Claude for substance.

## What is Grammarly?

Grammarly is an AI-powered writing assistant that checks grammar, spelling, clarity, and tone across virtually every writing surface — browsers, Microsoft Office, Google Docs, email clients, and desktop apps. Its GrammarlyGO feature adds generative AI capabilities for drafting and rewriting.

## Key Features

- **Grammar & style**: Catches grammar mistakes, wordiness, passive voice, and inconsistencies
- **Tone detection**: Analyzes how your message sounds and flags unintended tones
- **GrammarlyGO**: Generative AI for drafts, rewrites, and email replies
- **Clarity scores**: Rates your writing readability and suggests improvements
- **Plagiarism checker**: Compares against billions of web pages (Premium)
- **Brand tone**: Business plans let teams define a house style guide

## Hands-On Experience

I've used Grammarly for three years — before and after the AI writing revolution changed everything. Here's where it still earns its place:

**Inline corrections are still unmatched.** No other tool catches "affect vs effect", unnecessary passive voice, and comma splices in real-time inside Gmail as well as Grammarly does. This works while you type, without switching tools, inside every app I use. For someone writing dozens of emails a day, this passive correction saves real errors.

**Tone detection is underrated for team communication.** I've had Grammarly flag an email as "sounding harsh" before I sent it. The suggestion was right. This feature alone has saved a few awkward conversations.

**GrammarlyGO is where the value equation breaks down.** The generative AI features — drafting, rewriting, expanding — are noticeably weaker than Claude or GPT-4o. For anything beyond quick email replies, I open Claude instead.

**The pricing needs a rethink.** At $30/month for an individual, Grammarly Premium competes directly with Claude Pro and ChatGPT Plus — and loses on raw writing capability. It wins only on the "works everywhere without switching tabs" convenience.

## Pricing Breakdown

| Plan | Price | What You Get |

| Free | $0 | Basic grammar, limited suggestions |
| Premium | $30/mo | Full corrections, tone, GrammarlyGO, plagiarism |
| Business | $25/user/mo | Team features, style guide, analytics |

Annual Premium is ~$12/mo — much more reasonable.

## Who Should Use Grammarly?

- Non-native English speakers who need reliable grammar safety net
- Professionals sending high-volume email who need real-time tone checks
- Students who need plagiarism detection alongside writing help
- Anyone who wants passive AI correction without actively switching tools

## Alternatives to Consider

- **Claude / ChatGPT**: Better writing quality — but you have to actively open the tool
- **ProWritingAid**: Deeper style analysis, lower price — better for long-form writers
- **Hemingway Editor**: Free, excellent for clarity and readability — no AI drafting
- **LanguageTool**: Open-source alternative with good grammar checking, cheaper

## Final Rating: 3.8 / 5

Grammarly is still the best passive grammar correction layer that works everywhere. But the $30/month Premium price is hard to defend when annual pricing drops it to $12/mo — at that price, it's a reasonable complement to Claude or ChatGPT, not a replacement.

## FAQ

**Is Grammarly free?**
Yes — the free plan covers basic grammar and spelling. Advanced style, tone, and GrammarlyGO require Premium ($30/mo or ~$12/mo annual).

**Does Grammarly work with Google Docs?**
Yes — Grammarly has a browser extension that integrates with Google Docs, Gmail, and most web-based writing tools.

**Grammarly vs Claude for writing: which is better?**
Different tools. Grammarly passively corrects your writing as you type. Claude actively helps you draft, restructure, and improve content when you engage with it. Use both together for best results.

**Is Grammarly safe for confidential documents?**
Grammarly encrypts your data in transit and at rest. For highly sensitive legal or business documents, review their data processing terms — your text does pass through their servers.
