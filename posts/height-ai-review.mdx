## What is Height AI?
In the crowded landscape of productivity software, most "AI-powered" tools feel like thin wrappers around a standard API. Height AI stands out by embedding artificial intelligence directly into the project lifecycle. Instead of just adding a chat window to a spreadsheet-style task list, the developers have built an agentic system that understands task dependencies, project scopes, and team velocity.

As someone who has spent nearly two decades navigating CRM systems and sales operations, I have a low tolerance for "management bloat." Height AI aims to solve the primary friction point for devs: the time spent updating tickets, chasing status reports, and manually structuring backlogs. It essentially acts as a junior PM that lives inside your workspace, allowing you to convert chaotic slack threads or raw notes into actionable technical tasks.

## Hands-On Experience
I integrated Height AI into my workflow for a full sprint cycle, replacing my usual manual tracking system. Since I use Claude for almost all my coding and documentation tasks, I was curious to see if Height AI could handle the "management" layer better than my custom Claude-based prompts.

The first thing I noticed is the speed. I am a keyboard-first user, and Height’s command palette is snappy. When I pasted a raw transcript from a client call, the tool's AI successfully extracted four distinct features and three bug reports, correctly assigning them to the right categories without me needing to touch a mouse. This is where Height AI review metrics start to shine; the quality of the "segmentation" is significantly better than general-purpose tools like Notion AI.

However, it’s not perfect. There were moments when the AI tried to be "too smart." For instance, it once auto-assigned a deadline based on an assumption that I was using a standard agile sprint cadence, which I don’t. I had to go in and tweak the settings to force it to respect my specific project timeline. 

Compared to my daily driver, Claude, which is my go-to for complex logic and architectural review, Height AI is strictly a context-organizer. I still use Claude to draft the technical specs, but I now paste those final results into Height, where it takes over the execution tracking. It handles the "boring" part of my day exceptionally well. The AI isn't going to write your production code, but it makes sure that the code you write is actually aligned with the project roadmap.

For most mid-sized tech companies and startups, yes. It offers enough structure to handle complex roadmaps without the heavy administrative overhead that makes Jira difficult to maintain.

**2. Does Height AI integrate with my existing code repository?**

**3. Is my project data safe when using the AI features?**
Height uses LLMs to process task data, but they maintain strict data privacy standards. As with any AI tool, I recommend avoiding pasting sensitive, non-public API keys or proprietary trade secrets directly into the task description fields.
