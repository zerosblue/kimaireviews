## Quick Verdict

Midjourney V7 produces the most visually stunning AI images I've seen — full stop. If image quality is the priority and you can tolerate the Discord-based workflow, it's worth every dollar. For developers who just need functional UI mockups, cheaper alternatives exist.

## What is Midjourney?

Midjourney is an AI image generation service accessible via Discord. You submit text prompts (or image references) and receive high-resolution images in seconds. The latest model, V7, was released in early 2026 and introduces significant improvements in photorealism and character consistency.

## Key Features

- **V7 model**: Midjourney's most capable model — stunning detail and coherence
- **Character reference**: Keep the same character consistent across images with `--cref`
- **Style reference**: Apply the aesthetic of any image to new generations
- **Inpainting**: Edit specific parts of an image without regenerating everything
- **Aspect ratio control**: 1:1, 16:9, 9:16 and custom ratios
- **Zoom & pan**: Extend images beyond their original borders

## Hands-On Experience

I've used Midjourney for blog thumbnails, UI mockups, concept art, and social media graphics. Here's the honest verdict:

**The quality is unmatched**: Every time I show Midjourney outputs to non-technical colleagues, the reaction is the same — "wait, AI made that?" V7 handles lighting, texture, and composition at a level that still surprises me.

**The Discord workflow is a pain**: You're typing prompts in a busy chatroom. Finding your own images in a Discord channel with thousands of users is frustrating. The Midjourney website (midjourney.com/app) is improving but the Discord dependency remains.

**Text in images**: Midjourney still struggles with readable text. If you need "SALE" or a product name on an image, use Canva or Adobe Firefly instead.

**Character consistency**: The new `--cref` flag is a game-changer for anyone building visual stories or brand assets. You can finally keep the same "character" across 20 different images.

## Alternatives to Consider

## FAQ

**Is Midjourney free?**
No — Midjourney removed its free trial. Plans start at $10/month.

**What's the best Midjourney plan for beginners?**
The Basic plan ($10/mo) is a good start. Upgrade to Standard ($30/mo) if you're generating more than 200 images per month.

**Can I use Midjourney images commercially?**
Yes — on paid plans, you own the images you create. Read the full commercial usage terms at midjourney.com/docs/terms-of-service.

**Midjourney vs DALL-E 3: which is better?**
Midjourney produces more artistic and polished results. DALL-E 3 is more convenient (inside ChatGPT) and better follows exact text prompts.
