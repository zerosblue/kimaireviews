## What is Opus Clip?
In my 18 years of working in CRM and Sales Operations, I’ve learned that the biggest bottleneck to growth isn't generating leads; it’s the time spent manually chopping up content to stay visible. Opus Clip is an AI-powered video editing platform designed specifically to turn long-form videos—like webinars, podcasts, or tutorials—into short, punchy clips suitable for TikTok, YouTube Shorts, and Instagram Reels.

Unlike standard video editors where you’re scrubbing the timeline manually, Opus Clip uses an AI engine to analyze your video for "viral potential." As a solo developer, I don't have time to be a professional video editor, and Opus Clip fits into my workflow by handling the "grunt work" of video chopping.

## Hands-On Experience
I’ve been testing Opus Clip for about six months now. As a daily Claude user, I’m used to tools that are precise and efficient. I generally use Claude to refine the transcripts or generate unique hooks for the video, and then I feed the source footage into Opus Clip. 

The biggest hurdle for me has always been the "editing fatigue." When I upload a 45-minute technical deep dive, Opus Clip processes the video in roughly 10–15 minutes. The AI-generated clips are usually 70–80% ready. I find that the "Active Speaker" logic is significantly better than competitors, as it rarely glitches out when I’m demonstrating code snippets on screen. 

However, it isn't "set it and forget it." I still have to go into the editor to clean up the captions. I’ve noticed the AI occasionally misinterprets technical jargon specific to software development (e.g., misreading "API" or specific library names), which is expected but annoying. 

Compared to manual editing in Premiere Pro, Opus Clip saves me about three hours of work per long-form video. That is a massive win for a solo developer. However, if you are looking for complex, cinematic motion graphics, this isn't the right tool. It is a utility, not a creative studio. It excels at *volume* and *efficiency*.
