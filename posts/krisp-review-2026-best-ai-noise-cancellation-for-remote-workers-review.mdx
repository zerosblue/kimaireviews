## FAQ

### Does Krisp drain laptop battery life?
Because it runs real-time machine learning models locally, it does consume more CPU cycles than running naked audio. On modern Apple Silicon chips, the impact is negligible (around 2-4% CPU usage). On older Intel or AMD machines, you may notice a slight bump in fan noise during long meetings.

### Can I use Krisp with any microphone?
Yes. It is entirely hardware-agnostic. Whether you are using a high-end XLR studio mic through an audio interface, a cheap USB headset, or your laptop’s built-in array microphone, the software wraps around it cleanly.

### Is my meeting audio sent to external servers?
No. All noise cancellation processing happens locally on your device. For the optional transcription and meeting summary features, enterprise-grade encryption standards apply, but you can disable cloud-dependent features if your security policies demand total local isolation.

### How does the free tier limit work?
The free tier gives you 60 minutes of total noise cancellation per calendar day. Once you cross that threshold, the noise cancellation simply turns off until the next day, though your calls won't be disconnected. If you work remotely full-time, you will inevitably need to upgrade to the Pro tier.
